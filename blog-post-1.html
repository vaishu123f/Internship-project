New Library Wing Inaugurated

January 15, 2025 Admin Campus News
Back to Blog

State-of-the-Art Digital Library Opens Its Doors

We are thrilled to announce the grand inauguration of our new library wing, a modern facility designed to transform the learning experience for our students and faculty.

Modern Infrastructure

The new library wing spans over 15,000 square feet and features cutting-edge facilities that combine traditional learning resources with modern digital technology. The facility includes:

  • Extended Seating Capacity: Over 500 individual study spaces and 20 group study rooms equipped with smart boards and video conferencing facilities.
  • Modern Study Pods: 50 private study pods with ergonomic furniture, charging stations, and noise-canceling features for focused learning.
  • Digital Resources: Access to over 50,000 e-books, 200+ online databases, and thousands of academic journals from around the world.
  • 24/7 Access: Students can now access the library round the clock, providing flexibility for different learning schedules.
  • Smart Technology: RFID-based book management system, self-checkout kiosks, and AI-powered book recommendation system.

Special Features

The library features several innovative spaces designed to enhance the learning experience:

Reading Lounge

A comfortable reading area with natural lighting, comfortable seating, and a collection of popular fiction and non-fiction books for leisure reading.

Digital Lab

A dedicated computer lab with 100 high-performance workstations, specialized software for research, and high-speed internet connectivity.

Presentation Room

State-of-the-art presentation room with audio-visual equipment for group presentations, seminars, and workshops.

Impact on Students

The new library wing has already received overwhelming positive feedback from students. "The 24/7 access and modern facilities have completely transformed my study routine," says Priya Sharma, a third-year engineering student. "The quiet study pods are perfect for focused work, and the digital resources have made research so much easier."

Faculty members are equally excited about the new facilities. Dr. Rajesh Kumar, Head of the Computer Science Department, notes, "The new library provides our students with access to world-class resources and a learning environment that matches international standards."

Future Plans

This is just the beginning. The university plans to expand the digital collection further and introduce virtual reality learning zones in the coming months. We are committed to continuously enhancing our facilities to provide the best possible learning environment for our students.
